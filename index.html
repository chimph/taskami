# features
a planner, not another list.
‹dry now›

weather-aware planning

live WeatherKit data. outdoor tasks surface when it's dry and wait when it rains, with per-task wind and temperature limits. the ‹dry now› chip tells you why a task is up.

> plan

a real planning engine

two passes. hard filters cut the impossible: wrong area, not yet eligible, weather unmet, blocked. then a scoring pass ranks the rest by urgency, weather fit and importance, and packs the days ahead.

home · cabin · office

areas & locations

tasks belong to areas. each area gets its own weather, its own backlog and its own plan. arrive, and the right list is already waiting.

#chat

ember

ember is your friendly fire sprite assistant, powered by on-device AI, and your main way in. throw tasks at ember in plain words, like "fix the gate @home", and they're filed, shopping items too. then ask why something isn't on today, or say "take thursday off". code owns truth; ember owns the conversation.

// no account. no server.

local-first + iCloud

no tracking either. tasks live on your phone, sync through your own iCloud, and everything works offline. there's nothing to sign up for and nowhere for your data to leak.

# faq
Questions
When can I try it?
Join the waitlist below. Invites go out through TestFlight as spots open, with an App Store release to follow.
What platforms does it run on?
iPhone, including home screen and lock screen widgets. Dark mode only, by design.
How does weather planning work?
Tasks can require dry conditions, with optional wind and temperature limits, alongside an indoor/outdoor tag. The planner checks the forecast for each of your areas and packs outdoor work into the windows that fit, re-planning as the forecast shifts.
Where does my data live?
On your device, synced through your personal iCloud. No accounts, no third-party servers, and Ember, the assistant, runs on-device too.
